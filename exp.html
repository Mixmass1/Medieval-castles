Introduction

Today, these magnificent structures serve as living museums, drawing tourists and history enthusiasts alike to explore their rich heritage. Tourists can step back in time and immerse themselves in the stories of chivalry, courtly love, and medieval life that these castles hold within their walls. Here are some of the most eminent:

Malbork Castle

Location: Malbork, Poland
Constructed: 13th Century


Malbork Castle, which holds the distinction of being the castle, in the world based on its area is a magnificent marvel constructed by Teutonic knights in northern Poland around the 13th century. Its grand red brick Gothic architecture, walls and strategic positioning rendered it an impenetrable fortress that served as the command of the Teutonic Order. Today, recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site it stands as a captivating landmark that preserves the heritage of these knights while offering visitors a fascinating glimpse, into Polands medieval history.

Château de Doué-la-Fontaine

Location: Normandy, France
Constructed: 9th Century


The Château de Doué-la-Fontaine, located in Normandy, northern France, is the world's oldest known castle. Built by Normans, it follows the motte and bailey design. Despite enduring destruction, much of the fortress has survived, offering visitors a glimpse into its medieval past and the legacy of the Normans. Today, it stands as a living history book, preserving the rich heritage of the region.

Source

Neuschwanstein Castle

Location: Schwangau, Germany
Constructed: 19th Century


Neuschwanstein Castle, located in the region of Bavaria, Germany is an enchanting fortress that has become one of the most popular tourist destinations worldwide. King Ludwig II commissioned this castle in the century. Its architecture combines elements of medieval Romanesque and Gothic styles creating a fairy tale like atmosphere. The castles stunning setting amidst the Bavarian Alps surrounded by forests and serene lakes adds to its charm. Although King Ludwig II passed away before its completion visitors are still captivated by the halls and majestic chambers, within Neuschwanstein Castle. It stands as a symbol of Germanys cultural heritage while also serving as an inspiring representation of romanticism and imagination.

Mont Saint Michel Castle

Location: Normandy, France
Constructed: 9th Century


Saint Michel Castle , located on an island, is renowned for its unique tidal phenomenon. During the day, the water recedes, creating a causeway that connects it to the mainland. As the tides rise again, it becomes an island, making for a captivating sight. The castle's history, dating back to the medieval era, adds to its allure, and its blend of architectural styles showcases its significance. Today, as a UNESCO World Heritage Site, Mont Saint Michel continues to enchant visitors from around the world, leaving an indelible impression with its splendor and timeless beauty.

Edinburgh Castle

Location: Edinburgh, Scotland
Constructed: 12th Century


Edinburgh Castle, perched on Castle Rock, has a rich history as a royal residence and military base. After the Union with Great Britain, it remained a military stronghold. Today, it's a cherished tourist attraction, offering visitors a glimpse into Scotland's past and captivating them with its majestic charm.

Eltz Castle

Location: Wirschem, Germany
Constructed: 12th Century


Burg Eltz, built by the Eltz family near Koblenz, Germany, is a famous medieval castle. Its picturesque location and rich history attract tourists from around the world. With a blend of architectural styles, it stands as a symbol of Germany's cultural heritage and resilience. A visit to this UNESCO World Heritage Site offers an unforgettable journey through time, immersing visitors in the grandeur of the past.

Prague Castle

Location: Prague, Czechia
Constructed: 9th Century


Prague's colossal castle, one of the world's largest, stands by the Vltava River, connecting to the old town through the iconic Charles Bridge. Its rich history and mesmerizing architecture make it a must-visit destination, capturing the hearts of travelers and history enthusiasts alike.

Windsor Castle

Location: Winsdor, England
Constructed: 11th Century


Windsor Castle, steeped in British royalty and military history, was initially a fortress built by William the Conqueror. It later became a cherished residence for the royal family and served as a strategic military base. Today, it stands as a popular tourist attraction, captivating visitors with its splendid architecture and storied past as one of the official residences of the British monarchy.

Hohenschwangau

Location: Hohenschwangau, Germany
Constructed: 19th Century


Built on a 12th-century fortress by King Maximilian II, this castle stands proudly alongside Eltz and Neuschwanstein as one of Germany's most famous landmarks. Located in southern Bavaria, it attracts tourists from around the world with its rich history and captivating architecture. A symbol of royal grandeur, the castle offers stunning panoramic views and meticulously designed gardens, creating an enchanting experience for visitors.

Versaille

Location: Versaille, France
Constructed: 17th Century


Located near Paris it was a royal palace for the French royal family. It was the center of government and justice in France. It’s famous for its gardens and breathtaking interior. In 1789 it was no longer home to the royal family and in the 19th it became a history museum. About 15 million tourists visit the castle every year.
